Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Explore Sri Lanka's Ancient Rainforest Wilderness

Discover the breathtaking biodiversity of Sinharaja Forest Reserve with Grand Ceylon Holidays. This UNESCO World Heritage Site and International Biosphere Reserve is Sri Lanka's last viable area of primary tropical rainforest, spanning over 11,000 hectares of pristine wilderness.

Our expert-guided tours take you deep into this ecological treasure, where over 60% of the trees are endemic and many are considered rare. Sinharaja is a living laboratory of evolution, home to an extraordinary concentration of endemic species including birds, mammals, butterflies, insects, reptiles, and rare amphibians. The forest's name, which means "Lion King," reflects its status as a crown jewel of Sri Lanka's natural heritage.

As you trek through the dense forest canopy with our knowledgeable guides, you'll have the opportunity to spot rare wildlife such as the purple-faced langur, the endangered Sri Lankan leopard, and numerous endemic bird species that often move in mixed feeding flocksâ€”a phenomenon unique to Sinharaja. The forest is also home to over 50% of Sri Lanka's endemic species of mammals and butterflies, and 45% of all recorded reptiles.

Grand Ceylon Holidays offers carefully planned excursions that balance adventure with conservation awareness. Our guides are trained naturalists who share insights about the forest's complex ecosystem, ongoing research, and conservation efforts. Whether you're an avid birdwatcher, wildlife enthusiast, or simply seeking to experience one of the world's most remarkable rainforests, our Sinharaja tours provide an unforgettable journey into the heart of Sri Lanka's natural wonder, complete with comfortable accommodations at the forest edge and all necessary permits arranged in advance.
