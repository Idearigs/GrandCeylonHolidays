The Sacred City of Anuradhapura

Discover the ancient city of Anuradhapura, Sri Lanka's first capital and a UNESCO World Heritage Site with Grand Ceylon Holidays. Founded in the 4th century BC, Anuradhapura flourished for over 1,300 years as the center of Sinhalese civilization and Buddhism in Sri Lanka. Today, its well-preserved ruins offer a fascinating glimpse into the island's rich history and architectural achievements.

Explore magnificent dagobas (stupas), ancient pools, and sacred bodhi trees with our expert guides. The Sri Maha Bodhi, a sacred fig tree said to be grown from a cutting of the tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment, is the oldest historically authenticated tree in the world. Grand Ceylon Holidays offers carefully crafted tours that blend historical exploration with spiritual discovery in this remarkable ancient capital.
Key Attractions

  • Ruwanwelisaya - A magnificent white dagoba built in the 2nd century BC

  • Sri Maha Bodhi - The sacred Bo Tree, over 2,200 years old

  • Jetavanaramaya - Once the tallest stupa in the ancient world

  • Abhayagiri Monastery - Ancient Buddhist monastery complex

  • Isurumuniya Temple - Famous for its stone carvings and rock temple
