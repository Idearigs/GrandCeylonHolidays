The Golden Temple of Dambulla

Experience the awe-inspiring Dambulla Cave Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest and best-preserved cave temple complex in Sri Lanka with Grand Ceylon Holidays. Dating back to the 1st century BC, this remarkable site features five caves housing 153 Buddha statues, three statues of Sri Lankan kings, and four statues of gods and goddesses, including Vishnu and Ganesha.

The cave walls and ceilings are covered with intricate murals spanning 2,100 square meters, depicting Buddha's life and Sri Lankan history. Grand Ceylon Holidays offers expert-guided tours that reveal the spiritual significance and artistic mastery of this ancient sanctuary, allowing you to appreciate the remarkable preservation of these vibrant paintings and sculptures that have stood the test of time.
Key Features

  • Five distinct caves with unique religious and artistic significance

  • 153 Buddha statues in various poses and sizes

  • Over 2,100 square meters of stunning murals depicting Buddhist tales

  • Spectacular panoramic views of the surrounding countryside

  • Golden Temple at the base with a large golden Buddha statue
